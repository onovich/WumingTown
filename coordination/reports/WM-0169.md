## Architecture decision and speed audit

Direct source inspection established that current
`SetSpeedPayload.speed: 0 | 1 | 2 | 3` is an actual multiplier:
`GameSessionWorkerHost` advances the unchanged three ticks per 100-ms quantum
times that value and reports 0/30/60/90 TPS. Value 3 is not an ordinal that may
be relabelled 4x.

ADR-0018 therefore proposes:

- protocolVersion 1 and existing message families remain;
- envelope schema becomes 4 and default GameSession projection becomes v2;
- `SetSpeed` uses explicit `speedMultiplier: 1 | 2 | 4`;
- `Pause` remains a separate boolean, retains the requested multiplier and
  yields effective zero TPS;
- 1x/2x/4x map to 30/60/120 TPS and 3/6/12 integer ticks per 100-ms quantum;
- v2 rejects multipliers 0 and 3; unknown/newer schema/projection fails closed;
- WM-0172 atomically moves the default product to schema4/v2. Any v1 bridge is
  test/diagnostics-only, retains historical 0/1/2/3 behavior and is absent from
  the default module graph;
- GameSession save/load and public compatibility remain unsupported.

Projection-v2 additions are limited to PR-2 evidence: all eight activity
states, work kind, target, bounded route/cursor, integer/Q16 progress and state
ticks, decision owner versions, structured reason/suggestion and selected
resident need/schedule/capability facts. It adds no work-priority, direct-job,
selection, blueprint/build or save surface.

## Autonomous behavior and performance contract

The architecture fixes a 64x64 minimum scenario with eight residents and a
36,000-tick day. It composes needs, staggered shifts, capabilities, food,
rest/sleep, hauling, lamp patrol/refill and treatment through existing owner
stores, indexed candidates, JobCore drivers, path basis and ReservationLedger.

Each resident stores exactly one serializable activity state:
`idle`, `claiming`, `moving`, `working`, `blocked`, `completed`, `failed` or
`interrupted`. Structured reasons include owner/version basis, bounded scalar
parameters and read-only inspector suggestions. Suggestions do not execute a
command.

Hard decision caps are 24 visited candidates, 12 retained/scored candidates,
four exact paths, two new resident decisions per tick, 128 authoritative route
cells and a 32-cell projection window. The proposed reference budgets are
headless tick P95 <=4 ms/P99 <=8 ms, Web frame P95 <=16 ms, bounded scheduler
debt at 4x, no hot-loop allocation regression and zero long-run job/
reservation/route/queue/resource leak.

   evidence and an uninterrupted WebM plus raw bounded timeline.

   must identify concurrent work, follow a resident path/progress, understand
   idle/blocked/failed reasons and operate real controls; a separate reviewer
   verifies the result.

Raw Worker/debug payload, screenshot, implementer narration, pre-recorded clip
alone, internal handler calls or direct canvas-dispatched events cannot pass.

## Decisions and deviations

- Chose six serial tasks rather than a smaller nominally parallel DAG. Core
  autonomy, GameSession composition, public projection/Worker migration, client
  presentation, automated evidence and independent product acceptance have
  distinct ownership and safe integration gates. Width one is below the limit
  of three and avoids shared GameSession/protocol/default-route writers.
- Chose schema4/projection v2 because v1 cannot carry all PR-2 states, stable
  work kind, route/target, decision basis or actionable reason without UI
  synthesis.
- Chose explicit `speedMultiplier` and separate Pause after proving current 3
  is an actual multiplier. No relabelled 3x or zero-as-pause alias is allowed.
- WM-0172, not WM-0173, atomically cuts the default route to schema4/v2; v1 is
  test/diagnostics-only immediately after that integration.
- Accepted ADR-0017 after all implemented PR-1 repair/exit gates passed; no
  architecture content was rewritten.
- Created no PR-3+ implementation tasks and did not unblock legacy closeout.
