## Determinism, save, performance and security impact

Determinism: all selection ordering is score descending then stable fixture id; no real time, ambient randomness, UI state, Promise, Generator, coroutine, or closure-held job cursor is used.

Save/replay: no public save/schema/protocol changes. `RestJobDriverStore.createSnapshot` and `restoreFromSnapshot` expose an internal serializable numeric driver surface for focused replay work.

Review repair: `RestSleepReason` snapshot and trace serialization now explicitly accepts only known local rest/sleep reasons or known `ReservationReason` values. `reservation_entity_conflict` is covered by regression tests and no longer decodes to `rest.none`.

Performance: actor-side selection reads exact indexed buckets only. Focused measurement evidence records 30 total candidates, 24 visited, 12 retained, 4 exact path requests, 4 path results, 2 reservation claims per rest job, and 2 cleanup releases on terminal completion/failure/interruption. Metrics surfaces report selection count, candidate visits, reservation attempts, cleanup releases, completed/cancelled/interrupted counts, and dirty backlog/peak.

Security/boundaries: sim-core only; no DOM, React, Pixi, Electron, Node filesystem, Worker protocol, schema, package, lockfile, or dependency changes.
