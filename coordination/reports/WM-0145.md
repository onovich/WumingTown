## Owner blocker and self-correction

The Owner feedback is accepted as a hard product blocker:

- The current interface is overwhelming and hard to understand.
- The player cannot tell what they can do or what to focus on.
- The surface does not feel like RimWorld or even like a game.
- The UI does not explain how to build things or drive characters to work.
- A product surface where NPCs do not visibly move/act in a player-readable way
  is wrong for this genre.

My earlier acceptance framing was too engineering-heavy. I treated "select an
object and queue a shell-local action" as useful remediation evidence, while
underweighting whether a player can see a pawn accept work, move, execute,
finish or fail with a reason. That was the wrong product bar. WM-0145 resets
the bar to visible NPC action plus a real lamp/build command loop.

## Art-thread consultation incorporated

The second art-thread response from
`019f0e16-f987-7f33-8b28-a348d92b2179` is incorporated into the DAG:

- Current build must not enter public/EA/store/signing paths.
- Minimum experience should be a 2-3 minute "dusk lamp / simple build" slice.
- First screen must show the map as the subject, 2-4 active pawns, one clear
  objective, one obvious action and concise resources/alerts.
- Default UI must hide settings, diagnostics, product-gate/verdict/hash/source
  language and long explanatory panels.
- NPC action requires readable `idle`, `moving`, `working`, `blocked`,
  `completed` and failed states.
- Player command evidence must show command creation, job marker, pawn claim,
  pawn movement, work/progress and completion or structured failure reason.
- Low-fidelity dots/icons are acceptable; static status boards are not.
